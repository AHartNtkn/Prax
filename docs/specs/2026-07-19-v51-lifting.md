# v51 — Lifting leaves the engine: the □-lift is content; the census becomes the checker's net

Last of the extension rounds (and of the whole audit arc). The assessment's charge:
v48 means-tested the lifting tax without moving the responsibility — `Prax.Derive`,
the GENERAL derivation engine, still contains deontic vocabulary (`obligedHead`),
the deontic transformer (`liftObliged`), and an always-lifting string path; the
engine still decides WHEN to lift (`deonticProducible`, a producer census recomputed
on every retable, forcing the BUILD-ORDER INVARIANT on every world constructor).

REWRITTEN after the three-lens pre-gate panel (`.superpowers/sdd/v51-spec-review-*`):
the first draft mis-censused Feud (the same conflation the v48 panel corrected once
— "has deontic axioms" is orthogonal to "can produce obliged.*"; Feud is
census-FALSE, its feudPin documents the withheld lifted rows) [S-C1, C-C2]; the
panel replaced the transformer-list seam with plain composition [D-C1]; and it kept
the census alive as the checker's net rather than letting the forgot-to-declare
accident become silent [D-C2]. Findings folded below with [S]/[D]/[C] citations.

## The insight: which logic your axioms close under is an authored declaration

The paradigm (v44): engine responsibilities must be unrepresentable in world
content, and CONTENT responsibilities must not be engine magic. Closure under □ —
"obligations propagate through domain rules" (DEON property 1) — is a property of
a world's deontic logic, so the WORLD declares it; the engine neither knows the
vocabulary nor decides the moment. What v48 got right — no world that can invoke
obligation may silently lack the closure — survives as a CHECK rather than an
automatism: detection was the engine's act, and becomes the checker's net [D-C2].

## The design

- **`Prax.Derive` becomes deontics-free, its surface UNCHANGED except the Bool.**
  `liftObliged`, `obligedHead`, `cookAxioms`'s Bool, and `run`'s internal
  `mapMaybe liftObliged` DIE here. `cookAxioms :: [Axiom] -> [CookedRule]`;
  `run`/`closure`/`closureFrom`/`derived`/`contradiction` operate on exactly the
  axioms passed. There is NO transformer seam in the engine [D-C1: the panel
  proved a `[Axiom -> Maybe Axiom]` parameter strictly worse — it taxes ~15
  non-deontic call sites with `[]` noise to express what plain function
  composition already says].
- **`Prax.Deontic` owns its lift, as composition.**
  `obligedLift :: Axiom -> Maybe Axiom` (the moved `liftObliged`, verbatim
  semantics: all-Match bodies lift under `obliged.Obligor.`; anything else
  Nothing) and `obligedClose :: [Axiom] -> [Axiom]`
  (`axs ++ mapMaybe obligedLift axs` — exactly v48's expansion). A deontic world
  writes `setAxioms (obligedClose axs)`; `setAxioms` KEEPS its one-argument
  signature and every non-deontic call site is untouched. The head literal is
  drawn from ONE module constant that `obligationPath`, `obligationsOf`
  (Deontic.hs:151 — the hidden third `"obliged"` copy [D-I1]), and `obligedLift`
  all share.
- **`axioms st` holds the declared (expanded) list — named, not glossed [D-I2].**
  The field's meaning shifts from "authored axioms" to "the axiom set the world
  declared, closure included"; its Types.hs haddock (currently describing the
  dead v48 gate, Types.hs:412-424) is rewritten to say so. This conflation is
  load-bearing: the ViewInvariant reference path (`closure (axioms st)`) consumes
  the same declared set as the cooked path by definition. `retable` keeps
  deriving `cookedRules` (and footprint/axiomHeads/contMonotone) — but from the
  axiom list ALONE, census-free: `cookAxioms (axioms st0)`. What leaves retable
  is the gate, not the derivation; cookedRules remain a derived table of their
  source field, recomputed wherever every other derived table is.
- **The census MOVES to TypeCheck as the declaration-completeness net [D-C2].**
  `Relevance.deonticProducible` (the producer census: insert heads + wild-Call
  conservatism + the db leg) relocates into `Prax.TypeCheck` as the input to a
  new check: a world that CAN produce `obliged.*` facts, and whose axiom list
  contains a liftable axiom whose lifted twin (`obligedLift a`) is absent, flags
  a loud `DeonticUnclosed` TypeError naming the axiom and the fix
  (`Deontic.obligedClose`). This preserves v48's guarantee byte-for-byte — a
  producible world cannot silently lack DEON property 1 — while the lint's
  timing (typeCheck runs on the FINISHED world) is what kills the BUILD-ORDER
  INVARIANT: no setter-order sensitivity remains, because nothing at set time
  needs the census's answer. Worlds whose census is true but axiomless (Bar
  [S-C1 root cause]) are vacuously clean; census-false worlds with axioms
  (Feud) owe nothing. TypeCheck imports the Deontic constant (the checker
  importing a content module's vocabulary — the pre-v50 Rng/Script precedent).

## The world mapping, corrected [S-C1, C-C2]

Village is the ONLY lifter: census-true with axioms, `setAxioms (obligedClose …)`.
Feud is census-FALSE (kin/feud heads, no obliged producer, no wild Call — its

other worlds are axiomless; their census is moot. Every `setAxioms` call site in

## Verification

  Village deontic arcs (comply→owe→oblige) unchanged.
- `Deontic.obligedLift`/`obligedClose` pins at their new home: all-Match lifts,
  mixed-body refuses, the obliged.Obligor shape, obligedClose = base ++ lifts.

  an axiomless census-true world (Bar shape) is clean; a census-false world with
  axioms (Feud shape) is clean. RED-first per behavior.

  order v48 forbade) yields identical cookedRules and identical typeCheck to the
  setAxioms-outermost build.

  cooked implementation against the string implementation of the SAME declared

  Types.hs:412-424, docs/research/deon-notes.md:146-147, LEDGER capability rows
